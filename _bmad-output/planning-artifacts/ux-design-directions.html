Direction 1 Analysis
Layout Pattern:
Classic 3-pane: Sidebar (200px) + Email List (400px) + Thread Detail (flexible). This is the Gmail/Outlook pattern users expect.
Information Density:
Dense - compact email rows, visible confidence badges, AI chips inline. Power users can scan 20+ emails without scrolling.
AI Visibility:
Hybrid - AI chips/badges visible at-a-glance, "Why?" button reveals reasoning on-demand. Draft panel prominently highlights AI-generated content.
Pros:
  • Familiar pattern - zero learning curve
  • High information density for power users
  • Clear visual hierarchy (badges draw eye to priority)
  • Sidebar provides quick navigation
Cons:
  • May feel "traditional" vs innovative
  • Sidebar takes 200px - could use for content
  • Fixed email list width limits flexibility
Best For:
Users migrating from Gmail/Outlook who want familiar patterns with AI enhancements layered on top.

Direction 2 Analysis
Layout Pattern:
Single-pane focus with command palette overlay. No permanent sidebar - all navigation via ‚åòK. Thread opens as overlay or pushes current view.

Direction 3 Analysis
Layout Pattern:
Split-view 2-column. Left = Priority (urgent/important), Right = FYI/Handled. No sidebar - keyboard shortcuts (‚åò1/‚åò2) switch views.
Information Density:

Users who want immediate visibility into what needs attention vs what's handled autonomously. Trust through transparency.

Direction 4 Analysis
Layout Pattern:
Dashboard view with stats + action cards. Similar to Linear's issue view - each urgent email is a card with inline draft/actions.
Information Density:
Low - spacious cards prioritize urgent actions. Shows 2-3 urgent items prominently, others collapsed.
AI Visibility:
Very visible - AI drafts inline, reasoning cards, autonomous actions surfaced as separate section with review option.
Pros:
  • Dashboard metrics show impact (time saved, handled count)
  • Action-centric - every card has clear next step
  • AI transparency maximized (reasoning always visible)
  • Feels modern and product-focused (vs email-focused)
Cons:
  • Not a traditional "email client" - learning curve
  • Low density - can't see many emails at once
  • Dashboard approach may feel gimmicky if metrics not valuable
  • Requires scrolling to see all urgent items
Best For:
Users who want email as a task manager. Treats messages as actionable items, not a chronological list. Linear aesthetic.

Direction 5 Analysis
Layout Pattern:
Sidebar + timeline stream. Everything shown chronologically as events (received, AI draft, sent, archived). Like a Git history for email.
Information Density:
Medium - each event gets card treatment. Shows 4-5 events visible, but provides full context per event.
AI Visibility:
Explicit - AI actions are first-class timeline events. Shows "what happened when" including AI decisions.
Pros:
  • Chronological clarity - understand sequence of events
  • AI actions visible in timeline (not hidden)
  • Narrative flow - tells story of your email day
  • Color-coded timeline dots show priority at glance
Cons:
  • Not optimized for "inbox zero" workflow
  • Mixed events (sent/received/AI) may be confusing
  • Requires scrolling to see earlier events
  • Can't easily "batch process" emails
Best For:
Users who want to understand their email activity holistically. Emphasizes transparency and audit trail over efficiency.

Direction 6 Analysis
Layout Pattern:
Single-email focus mode. Only one email visible at a time. Navigate via J/K keys like Superhuman. Zero chrome.
Information Density:
Minimal - one email gets full screen attention. Progress indicator shows position (1/47). Forces serial processing.
AI Visibility:
Minimal - AI draft shown as collapsed card, press Space to expand. "?" reveals reasoning. Keyboard shortcuts for all actions.
Pros:
  • Zero distraction - absolute focus on one email
  • Fastest keyboard workflow (Superhuman DNA)
  • Forces decisions (can't scan and defer)
  • Pristine aesthetic - feels premium
Cons:
  • Can't scan multiple emails quickly
  • No spatial context (where am I in inbox?)
  • Requires strict keyboard discipline
  • May feel too constraining for some workflows
Best For:
Users who want inbox zero via serial processing. Superhuman users who value speed and focus over flexibility.
